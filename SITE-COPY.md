## 0. Direction (what changed and why)

**Architecture.** The forced "Личное / Бизнесу" split-screen entrance is gone.
The home page is now one thing: **a B2B company introduction** that earns a new
corporate client's trust and a request for a quote. No fork in the road, no
"choose your side" — a visitor lands and immediately understands *who Folk Print
is, what it makes, and why a company would order from it.*

**B2C is relegated.** Personal/one-off orders stay available but quiet: a single
calm line near the footer — *"Печатаете для себя или в подарок?"* → a link to the
personal page. It is never a co-equal headline. The business is corporate merch;
the site should say so without apology.

**Photography.** Placeholder boxes and the client's own phone snaps are not
acceptable as final art. Source **professional, consistent imagery**: real
branded apparel worn by teams (not stock-faces — Tashkent companies, real
fits), flat-lays of a full kit (hoodie + tee + cap + tote in one brand colour),
macro close-ups that *show the craft* (the ridge of an embroidered logo, the
ink sitting in silkscreen, DTF colour depth), and a few honest workshop frames
(press, embroidery machine, a folded, packed order). Warm light, the cream/ink/
amber palette, shallow depth of field. Every photo should make a procurement
manager think *"these people are serious."*

**Typography.** The current type is too small and too timid. Go **big and
confident**: Oswald for display set LARGE (heroes 56–96px desktop), Manrope for
body at a comfortable 17–18px with generous line-height (1.55–1.65). Tight,
purposeful headlines; roomy, readable paragraphs. White space is not empty
space — it's how the work looks expensive.

## 7. Как мы работаем (process) — #process

**Section label:** Как работаем

**H2:** От заявки до тиража — пять шагов

**Steps:**

1. **Заявка и бриф** — Рассказываете задачу: что, сколько, к какому сроку.
   Отвечаем в тот же рабочий день.
2. **Макет и расчёт** — Готовим визуализацию на изделии и прозрачную смету.
   Правки — пока не понравится.
3. **Образец** — Делаем эталонный экземпляр. Видите и трогаете результат до
   запуска партии.
4. **Производство** — Печатаем или вышиваем тираж на своём оборудовании, с
   контролем на каждом этапе.
5. **Выдача и документы** — Складываем, упаковываем, передаём тираж и полный
   пакет документов.

## 12. Voice rules (the part that keeps it from sounding like AI)

**Do:**
- Talk to **one person** — a marketing / HR / procurement lead who has to order
  branded apparel and not get burned. Address their actual fear: bad quality,
  blown deadlines, no documents.
- Be **specific and concrete.** "Образец до тиража", "счёт, акт, накладная",
  "свои станки" beat any adjective. Specifics are what sound expensive.
- Keep a **confident, warm, slightly proud** tone — a craftsman who knows the
  work is good and doesn't need to shout it.
- Vary rhythm: a short, punchy line, then a fuller one. Read it aloud — if it's
  flat, rewrite it.

**Don't (these are the exact things that read as "basic AI"):**
- ❌ "Качественная печать по доступным ценам"
- ❌ "Индивидуальный подход к каждому клиенту"
- ❌ "Команда профессионалов своего дела"
- ❌ "Широкий ассортимент продукции"
- ❌ "Гибкая система скидок"
- ❌ "Мы ценим каждого клиента" / "Ваш успех — наш успех"
- ❌ Three adjectives where one verb would do.
- ❌ Any claim we can't back up (fake stats, methods we don't run, an email we
  don't have, contracts if we don't actually issue them).

**The test:** if a competing print shop in Tashkent could paste the same
sentence onto their site unchanged, delete it and write the one only Folk Print
could say.
